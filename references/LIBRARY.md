## Session-Notation Library (Copy/Paste)

Diese Library ist bewusst **modell-agnostisch** und als **Baustein-Sammlung** gedacht. Kombiniere pro Abschnitt typischerweise 2–6 Tags (Prioritäten!).

### Quick-Index (Suche)

- **Header/DNA**: A
- **Sections/Timeline**: B
- **Drums/Groove**: C
- **Bass/808**: D
- **Instrument-Layering**: E
- **Vocal-Regie**: F
- **Mix/Welt**: G
- **FX/Automation/Transitions**: H
- **Negative Space**: I
- **Motifs**: J
- **Checks**: K
- **Hit Points / Sync Map**: L
- **Mixing-Basics (Einsteiger)**: M
- **Rezepte (fertige Mini-Blöcke)**: N

### Schreibkonventionen (empfohlen)

- **Ein Tag pro Zeile**: stabiler als „alles in einen Block“.
- **Regie in `[...]`, Lyrics ohne Klammern**.
- **Timing-Konvention**:
  - Standard ist **4/4**, wenn nicht anders angegeben.
  - **Bars** zählen standardmäßig **pro Abschnitt** (Verse/Hook/Bridge). Für globale Zählung schreibe „Song bars …“.
  - **Beat-Notation**: `downbeat 1`, `beat 2`, `beat 4`, `“&” of 3` (Offbeat), `pickup 1/2 bar`.
- **Bar-Phasen pro Abschnitt**: `Bars 1–4`, `Bars 5–8`, plus gezielte Hits: `bar 8`.
- Trennzeichen:
  - `|` für parallele Infos (Layer + Groove + Mix)
  - `;` für Unterpunkte
- **Zahlen sind optional**: Wenn unsicher, nutze relative Begriffe („very low“, „subtle“, „wide“).

## A) Header / Song-DNA Tags

```text
[Title: "..."]
[Genre/Style: ...]
[Key: ...]
[Meter: 4/4]
[Tempo: ... BPM]
[Swing/Groove: ...]
[Feel: nocturnal | triumphant | intimate | ...]
[Drums: ...]
[Bass: ...]
[Instruments: ...]
[Vocal: ...]
[Space/Texture: ...]
```

Beispiele:

```text
[Tempo: 80 BPM | Swing: 56%]
[Drums: tight trap kit; crisp snare/clap on 2&4; hats swung 1/8 w/ 1/16 flurries]
[Space/Texture: small room | tape hiss low | vinyl crackle subtle]
[Width: mono→wide 120% by bar 7]
```

---

## B) Sections / Timeline Labels

```text
[Intro — set the scene]
[Verse 1 — restrained pocket]
[Pre-Hook — small lift]
[Hook — mantra hook | tight + serious]
[Verse 2 — same pocket | more tension]
[Bridge — contrast / breakdown]
[Final Hook — final lift]
[Outro — let it breathe]
```

---

## C) Drums / Groove / Pocket (Mikrosteuerung)

```text
[Bars 1–4: bass + soft snare + kick only | hats ghosted]
[Kick: busier bars 3–4 & 7–8; ghost kick −12 dB on “&” of 3]
[Hats: swung 1/8; 1/16 flurries on bar 4; triplet burst every 2 bars]
[Snare: rim-click texture joins Verse 2; clap layer only in Hook]
[Switch: halftime for 4–8 bars; pull hats; snap back into Hook]
[Drop: remove kick 1 bar pre-Hook (breath) | keep room tone]
```

---

## D) Bass / 808 (Drive, Glue, Transitions)

```text
[Bass: 2-bar ostinato loop; remains constant as anchor]
[808: long sustain; slides on bars 4 & 8; glide up 2 semitones into each Hook]
[808: swell −∞→−6 dB into Hook | then tighten release]
[Bass: mute first 2 bars of Verse; re-enter bar 3 with mild saturation]
```

---

## E) Instrument-Layering (Call/Response, Glue, Lift)

```text
[Bars 5–8: add gentle piano/Rhodes chords | same groove]
[Instruments: staccato violin chops (call) | airy panflute (response) | lush harp arps]
[Guitar: muted comping | very low for glue]
[Pads: glassy pad for bed | widen in final Hook]
[Sax fills: subtle between vocal lines | keep under lead]
```

---

## F) Vocal-Regie (Einstieg, Verhalten, Harmonien)

```text
[Vocal: lead, spoken, close mic, intimate]
[Vocal: calm baritone | restrained | minimal vibrato]
[Vocal: rap pocket tight | consonants crisp | low grit]
[Hook: mantra delivery | serious | centered]
[Harmonies: low harmony on last word each line]
[Hook stack: add higher harmony on line 2 | fuller on repeat]
[Ad-libs: sparse; only last Hook (“yeah”, “don’t stop”, “let it ride”)]
```

Hinweis: Lyrics danach **ohne** `[...]`.

---

## G) Mix / Klangbild / Welt (Texturen, Raum, Breite)

```text
[Space: small room | short verb | dry lead]
[Texture: tape hiss | vinyl crackle 3% | subtle room tone]
[Width: mono→wide 120% by bar 7 | ad-libs L/R −16 dB]
[Atmos: distant crowd/sea breeze −20 dB | room tone constant]
[Glue: guitar/pad very low | “feels like one room”]
```

---

## H) FX / Automation / Transitions (Cuts, Risers, Hits)

```text
[Filter: low-pass 300→8k over 8 bars]
[Reverse vox tail: 1/2 bar into Hook]
[Riser: white-noise −18→−9 dB into Hook]
[Verb throw: 25% one-shot on last clap pre-Hook]
[Tape-stop: 1 beat on bar 8]
[Backspin: short into bar 9]
[Stutter: 1/4-note on first syllable of target word]
[Laser ping: L/R on keyword hit]
```

---

## I) Negative Space / „Beat breathes“ (Kontrolle durch Weglassen)

```text
[Breath space: hats mute bar 8 | keep bass + room tone]
[Breakdown: 4 bars bass + keys only | close mic vocal]
[Drop-out: remove drums for 2 bars; bring back with fill]
[Outro: drums mute last 2 bars | delay tail 3s | vinyl pop end]
```

---

## J) Motifs (Wiederholbare Signaturen)

```text
[Motifs to repeat each Hook]
[reverse vox tail 1/2 bar pre-downbeat]
[808 glide up 2 semitones into 1]
[kick gets busier bars 3–4 & 7–8]
[bar 8 tape-stop (1 beat) every 2nd Hook]
```

---

## K) Qualitäts-Checks (kurz)

- **Lyrics sind nie in `[...]`**.
- Pro Abschnitt **max. 3–5 Prioritäten** (sonst verwässert).
- Bar-Referenzen sind **lokal verständlich** (innerhalb des Abschnitts).
- Keine **Widersprüche** in Space/Lead (z. B. „dry lead“ vs „huge hall“ gleichzeitig).

---

## L) Hit Points / Sync Map (WANN etwas passiert)

Ziel: **Beat und Lyrics aufeinander „einrasten“ lassen** (Instrument/FX startet *genau* auf einem Schlag oder trifft *genau* ein Wort).

Wichtig: Das **Zielwort steht in den Lyrics**, nicht in `[...]`. In `[...]` referenzierst du es nur als Marker.

### Beat-Anker (zeitliche Marker)

```text
[On downbeat 1: ...]
[On beat 3: ...]
[On “&” of 3: ...]
[Bar 8: ...]
[Last beat of bar 8: ...]
[Pickup: 1/2 bar pre-Hook: ...]
```

### Word-Anker (Lyrics als Triggerpunkte)

```text
[On word: "spiral" (Hook line 2): drop hats for 1 beat]
[On word: "down": tape-stop 1 beat | resume next bar]
[On word: "breathe": harmony swell + cymbal shimmer]
[On last word each line: low harmony hit]
```

### Kombi-Beispiele (Copy/Paste)

```text
[Bar 8: tape-stop 1 beat on downbeat | backspin into bar 9]
[On “&” of 4 (pre-Hook): reverse vox tail 1/2 bar]
[On word: "now": stutter 1/4-note (first syllable) | kick mutes 1 beat]
[On snare hit (2 & 4): laser ping subtle L/R]
```

## M) Grundeffekte & Mixing-Basics (für Einsteiger)

Diese Sektion ist dafür da, dass man **ohne Audio-Vokabular** trotzdem gezielt prompten kann. Denk in drei Fragen:

- **Was** soll passieren? (z. B. „Filter runter“, „Echo nur am Zeilenende“)
- **Wann**? (Bar/Beat/Word-Anker)
- **Wie** fühlt es sich an? (subtle / obvious, warm / cold, tight / washed)

### M1) Filter / EQ-Moves (Tonhöhe/Brillanz als Bewegung)

**Wofür:** Spannung aufbauen, Intro/Breakdowns „unter Wasser“, dann aufreißen.

```text
[Filter: low-pass 300→8k over 8 bars]
[Filter: low-pass closes to 1k in last 2 bars pre-Hook; opens on downbeat]
[EQ move: tuck harsh highs on hook; brighten ad-libs only]
```

### M2) Reverb (Raum) & „Verb Throw“ (nur auf einzelne Hits)

**Wofür:** Lead trocken/intim lassen, aber einzelne Wörter/Claps „in den Raum werfen“.

```text
[Space: dry lead | short room verb]
[Verb throw: 25% one-shot on last word of each Hook line]
[Verb throw: on snare pre-Hook only; then back to dry]
```

### M3) Delay / Echo (Tail, Ping-Pong, Call/Response)

**Wofür:** Enden verlängern, Rhythmus betonen, Hook größer machen ohne Matsch.

```text
[Delay: short slap on lead (subtle)]
[Delay: ping-pong on ad-libs only | keep lead centered]
[Delay tail: 3s on Outro; then cut]
```

### M4) Saturation / Distortion (Wärme, Körnung, Aggression)

**Wofür:** 808/Drums „griffig“, Vocal „Grit“, Lo‑Fi‑Vibe.

```text
[Saturation: mild tape warmth on drums bus]
[Distortion: subtle grit on hook vocal; clean verses]
[808: light saturation in Hook; tighten release]
```

### M5) Compression / Sidechain (Pump/Glue/Atmung)

**Wofür:** Beat atmet, Kick gewinnt Platz, Mix wirkt „zusammen“.

```text
[Glue: gentle bus compression feel (not squashed)]
[Sidechain: bass ducks lightly to kick (subtle pump)]
[Drop: remove pump in breakdown; return in Hook]
```

### M6) Stereo / Width / Panning (Breite als Arrangement)

**Wofür:** Hook größer, Verse enger; Elemente trennen ohne Lautstärke.

```text
[Width: verse narrow | hook wider]
[Width: mono→wide 120% by bar 7]
[Pan: ad-libs L/R low | lead centered]
```

### M7) Pitch / Formant / Auto-Tune‑Feel (Vocal-Charakter)

**Wofür:** modern/clean vs. raw/natural, Hook „glänzt“.

```text
[Vocal: natural tuning | intimate]
[Vocal: light auto-tune feel in Hook; less in Verse]
[Formant: slightly lower for spoken intro (subtle)]
```

### M8) Time FX: Stutter / Chop / Glitch (rhythmische Effekte)

**Wofür:** Betonung auf ein Wort, Energie-Switch, „DJ edit“-Momente.

```text
[On word: "up": stutter 1/4-note (first syllable)]
[Chop: vocal cut into 1/8 slices for 1 bar; then normal]
[Glitch: micro-repeat last syllable of hook line 4 (subtle)]
```

### M9) DJ/Transport FX: Tape-Stop / Backspin / Reverse

**Wofür:** Abschnittsgrenzen „markieren“, Hook-Entry dramatisieren.

```text
[Bar 8: tape-stop 1 beat; resume next bar]
[Backspin: short into bar 9]
[Reverse vox tail: 1/2 bar pre-downbeat into Hook]
```

### M10) Risers / Impacts (Übergänge & „Lift“)

**Wofür:** Pre→Hook, Bridge→Final Hook, Drops ankündigen.

```text
[Riser: white-noise −18→−9 dB into Hook]
[Impact: subtle hit on downbeat 1 of Hook]
[Pickup: 1/2 bar pre-Hook: riser + reverse tail]
```

### M11) Noise/Texture (Lo‑Fi, Film, „Welt“)

**Wofür:** Identität, Kohärenz, Raumgefühl auch bei wenigen Instrumenten.

```text
[Texture: tape hiss low]
[Texture: vinyl crackle subtle | occasional vinyl pop]
[Atmos: distant crowd/sea breeze very low | room tone constant]
```

### M12) Gating / Drops / Mutes (Klarheit durch Weglassen)

**Wofür:** Punch, Fokus, „breath space“, damit Hook stärker wirkt.

```text
[Breath space: hats mute bar 8 | keep bass + room tone]
[Gate: cut reverb tails in Verse; open in Hook]
[Drop: remove kick 1 bar pre-Hook; return with fill]
```

## N) Rezepte (fertige Mini-Blöcke)

Diese Mini-Blöcke kannst du 1:1 in einen Abschnitt kopieren und danach anpassen.

### N1) „Small lift“ (Pre → Hook)

```text
[Pre — small lift | breath space]
[background: soft wordless hum | cymbal shimmer]
[Pickup: 1/2 bar pre-Hook: reverse vox tail]
```

### N2) „Hook lands hard“ (klarer Entry)

```text
[Hook — pocket locks]
[Impact: subtle hit on downbeat 1 | width opens]
[Kick: busier bars 3–4 & 7–8 (of this Hook phrase)]
```

### N3) „Intimate breakdown“ (Bridge)

```text
[Bridge — intimate breakdown]
[4 bars: drop drums; keep bass + keys | close mic vocal]
[Space: dry lead | room tone present]
```

### N4) „Micro-switch“ (letzte 2 Bars vor Section-Change)

```text
[Micro-switch: Bars 7–8 (of this section): filter closes; hats mute bar 8]
[On downbeat 1 of next section: filter opens | drums snap back]
```

### N5) „DJ-marked transition“ (bar 8 Hit)

```text
[Bar 8: tape-stop 1 beat on downbeat | backspin into bar 9]
[Reverse vox tail: 1/2 bar pre-downbeat into next Hook]
```

## O) Mini-Glossar (für Nicht-Audio-Leute)

Diese Begriffe helfen beim Suchen/Prompten, ohne „Mixing-Sprache“ zu können:

- **downbeat**: der „1“-Schlag im Takt (Startpunkt)
- **pickup**: kurzer Anlauf *vor* dem Abschnitt (z. B. 1/2 bar pre-Hook)
- **offbeat / “&”**: Zwischen-Schläge (z. B. „& of 3“)
- **ghost note**: sehr leise Zusatznote (fühlt sich groovy an, ohne laut zu sein)
- **fill**: kurzes Übergangs-Lick (meist Drums) in den letzten Beats/Bars
- **drop**: bewusstes Wegnehmen von Elementen (z. B. Kick raus) für Spannung
- **lift**: Abschnitt wirkt größer (mehr Breite, mehr Layer, mehr Energie)
- **stack**: mehrere Vocal-Harmonien/Layer gleichzeitig
- **throw**: Effekt nur auf *ein* Hit/Wort (z. B. „verb throw“)
- **glue**: Elemente wirken „zusammen“ (oft durch leichte Kompression/Room)

## P) Intensity-Skala (statt Zahlen)

Wenn du keine dB/%/ms angeben willst:

```text
[FX intensity: subtle]
[FX intensity: medium]
[FX intensity: hard]
```

Beispiel:

```text
[Verb throw: medium on last word of Hook lines]
[Saturation: subtle tape warmth on drums]
[Tape-stop: hard, 1 beat on bar 8]
```
